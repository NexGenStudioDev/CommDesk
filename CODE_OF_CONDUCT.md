# Code of Conduct

Version: 1.0
Effective date: 2026-03-12

## Purpose

CommDesk is committed to a respectful, professional, and harassment-free
community for everyone. This Code of Conduct defines expected behavior,
unacceptable behavior, and how reports are handled.

## Community Commitment

We welcome participants regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity,
gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and professionally.
- Focus feedback on ideas, code, and outcomes, not individuals.
- Assume good intent while still asking clarifying questions.
- Accept constructive feedback and respond constructively.
- Respect different viewpoints, backgrounds, and communication styles.
- Keep discussions relevant to the project and its goals.
- Help maintain a safe, inclusive, and collaborative environment.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not allowed:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or stalking.
- Hate speech, discriminatory language, or derogatory comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Personal attacks, insults, humiliation, or hostile baiting.
- Trolling, flaming, deliberate provocation, or repeated disruption.
- Sharing private or identifying information without explicit permission.
- Impersonation, misrepresentation, or deliberate misinformation.
- Spam, unsolicited advertising, or repeated off-topic posting.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.
- Any behavior that could reasonably make others feel unsafe.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers and moderators are expected to:

- Enforce this policy fairly and consistently.
- Prioritize community safety over status or technical contribution level.
- Document incidents and decisions with clear reasoning.
- Respect privacy and limit sharing of sensitive details.
- Recuse themselves from decisions where there is a conflict of interest.

## Scope

This policy applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and code reviews.
- Repository documentation, comments, and linked community channels.
- Any external space where an individual is representing this project.

## Reporting Process

To report behavior that may violate this Code of Conduct:

- Use: <https://github.com/NexGenStudioDev/CommDesk/issues>
- Include: what happened, when it happened, where it happened, and any
  supporting evidence (screenshots, links, logs).

If there is an immediate risk of harm, contact local emergency services first.

## Report Handling and Privacy

Maintainers will:

- Acknowledge reports within 72 hours when possible.
- Review reports promptly and assess severity.
- Keep reporter identity and sensitive details as confidential as possible.
- Share information only with people required to investigate and resolve.
- Take action based on available evidence and community safety.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Actions are determined by impact, intent, frequency, and prior incidents.

1. Correction
 - Used for minor first-time issues.
 - Action: guidance and request to stop behavior.
2. Warning
 - Used for significant or repeated issues.
 - Action: formal warning and clear behavior expectations.
3. Temporary Restriction
 - Used for serious misconduct or repeated violations.
 - Action: temporary mute, interaction limits, or suspension.
4. Permanent Ban
 - Used for severe abuse, threats, discrimination, doxxing, or ongoing
   harmful behavior.
 - Action: permanent removal from project spaces.

## Appeals

If someone believes an enforcement action was incorrect, they may request a
review by opening a new issue and referencing the original decision. Appeals
must include additional context or evidence. Final decisions are made by the
maintainer team.

## Good-Faith Reporting

Reports made in good faith are protected. Knowingly false or malicious reports
may themselves result in enforcement action.

## Changes to This Policy

Maintainers may update this document as the project grows. Significant updates
should be announced in repository discussions, release notes, or other project
communication channels.

## Acknowledgment

This policy is informed by common open-source community standards, including
the Contributor Covenant.
